Nyatapola Temple

Namaste! You're looking at the beautiful Nyatapola temple in the above given picture and beautiful natural hills on the background as well. Nyatapola Temple is a well known temple all around the country and one of the major attractions for tourist in Bhaktapur. Lots of tourists visit the city to catch a glimpse of this beautful temple which is also the tallest temple of Nepal. It has been granted the status of UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Nyatapola temple was built by the then King of Nepal Bhupatindra Malla in 1708 AD. It is believed that he himself carried three bricks on his shoulder while laying the foundation of the temple. It is also said that Nyatapola temple was built in a very stable foundation which was proved after the 2015 AD earthquake in Nepal as the temple didn't suffer any serious damage during the earthquake.

Bhaktapur Durbar Square

Bhaktapur Durbar Square was the place where the past Kings of Bhaktapur used to stay. Bhaktapur Durbar Sqaure has a lot of ancient architecture which attracts a lot of external as well as internal tourists.

It was originally built by King Jitamitra Malla in 1696 AD and later King Bhupatindra Malla reconstructed the sites back in late 17th or early 18th century.

55 Windows Palace

It was originally built by King Jitamitra Malla in 1696 AD and later King Bhupatindra Malla reconstructed the sites back in late 17th or early 18th century. It was constructed after the successful construction of the five storied (Nyatapola) which was completed in a very short period. The Palace, the medieval period architecture was built by the local crasftsmen and builders using the then local technologies using local materials.

The Palace were built in a specially made bricks, such as, Maappa and Dachiappa with mud mortar of high quality. The floors, beams, windows and doors were made of different types of the then available timbers, such as, pine, sal, teak and other types. Special roofing tiles (Jhingate) were used to cover the sloped roofs on timber supports.
